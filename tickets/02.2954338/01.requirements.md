## Constraints

- Least-privilege: do not grant broader roles than what the service actually needs.
- Must align with GCP IAM best practices.
- Role assignments must be environment-aware (no prod-level roles on dev).
- Per TCCC/C4P policy: do not issue admin access to developers or tech leads across GCP environments.
- Deployment permissions must be modeled through CI/CD service accounts (WIF/service-account path), not broad human-user admin grants.
- Preserve source artifact style: future AI additions should match the structure and writing style already established in the permissions matrix by James.
- Use ngps-global as migration-reference evidence, not as a direct permission template for GCP operations.

## Non-Goals

- Terraform implementation of the roles (separate ticket).
- Other services in the same permissions matrix file beyond NGPS/Promo+.
- Individual user-to-group provisioning (deciding who is added to each AD group) — role definitions for each group are in scope; the provisioning act is not.
- Reproducing AWS service access patterns one-to-one in GCP without migration validation.

## Reviewer Focus

- Are predefined roles truly least-privilege, or do they grant excess permissions?
- Are any custom roles actually needed, or can predefined roles cover all cases?
- Is the environment differentiation correct and consistent?
- Does the scope snapshot include all operator-critical foundations needed for day-to-day Admin operation?
- Do role assignments respect policy by avoiding admin grants to developers/tech leads and keeping deployment elevation on service accounts?
